## Bangladesh Property Market — What You Should Know

### Market Size & Growth

Bangladesh's real estate sector is one of the country's most significant economic drivers, contributing an estimated **7.5–8.6% of GDP** and supporting millions of jobs in construction and related industries. The residential market alone is projected to reach **US$2.08 trillion** in value by the end of 2025, with the broader real estate sector forecast to grow at a **CAGR of 7.9% through 2031**.

After a challenging first half of 2025 — marked by inflationary pressure and tighter financing — the market stabilised by mid-year, with developers adapting through flexible payment plans and more transparent pricing.

---

### The Two Markets We Work In

#### Dhaka
The undisputed centre of Bangladeshi real estate. With a population expected to exceed **30 million by 2035**, demand for quality housing in Dhaka is structural, not cyclical. Key dynamics:

- **Premium zones** — Gulshan, Banani, and Baridhara command **BDT 18,000–35,000 per sqft** and attract NRBs (Non-Resident Bangladeshis), diplomats, and corporate executives.
- **Mid-market growth** — Bashundhara R/A, Uttara, and Mirpur offer strong value with growing amenities and improving connectivity.
- **Infrastructure effect** — The Dhaka Metro Rail (MRT), Elevated Expressway, and Padma Bridge have measurably increased values in previously peripheral areas like Purbachal, Keraniganj, and Savar.
- **Supply trend** — 48% of listed properties are under construction; high-rise apartments now dominate new development as land scarcity pushes vertical growth.

#### Chattogram
Bangladesh's primary port city and commercial engine. Chattogram accounts for roughly **14% of total national real estate transactions**, with strong demand for both mid-tier residential and mixed-use commercial developments. Prime areas — GEC Circle, Agrabad, and Nasirabad — command premium rents and sale prices. Proximity to the Bay of Bengal also drives industrial warehousing demand.

---

### What Buyers Are Looking For in 2025

Buyer priorities have shifted noticeably. Beyond square footage, purchasers now weigh:

- **Legal transparency** — RAJUK-compliance, verified titles, and clear registration pathways
- **Sustainability** — Energy-efficient buildings, rainwater harvesting, solar common areas, and LEED-aligned construction are increasingly sought after
- **Amenities** — Backup generator, lift, security, and community facilities are near-standard expectations in Dhaka's mid-to-upper segments
- **Developer track record** — Timely handovers and after-sales support have become decisive purchase factors following a period of market uncertainty
- **Connectivity** — Proximity to metro stations, expressways, and access roads now features prominently in buyer decision-making

### Challenges to Know

- **Land scarcity** in central Dhaka keeps prices high and drives vertical development
- **Registration bureaucracy** can be slow; professional guidance (like SB Properties) is strongly recommended
- **Flood and drainage risk** varies significantly by neighbourhood — a due-diligence factor we assess for every listing
- **Construction cost volatility** affects developer timelines; phased payment plans are now common
- **Financing** — Home loan rates currently sit at approximately 7–9%, with government-backed schemes available for qualifying buyers

---

### NRB Investment

Non-Resident Bangladeshis represent a significant buyer segment. Bangladesh received **USD 24.73 billion in remittances in FY2023**, a substantial portion of which flows into Dhaka property — particularly in Gulshan, Banani, and Baridhara. Improved digital platforms and clearer ownership rules have made remote purchasing more accessible. SB Properties supports NRB clients through document guidance and remote viewings.

## Features

- **Hero search panel** — Location, budget, and home-type filters scroll to listings on submit
- **Listing filter** — Client-side JS filters cards by Duplexes / Apartments without a page reload
- **Contact form** — Validates and confirms submission with an inline status message
- **Fully responsive** — Three breakpoints: desktop (1200px+), tablet (900px), mobile (640px)
- **Accessible** — Semantic HTML5, ARIA labels, live regions, and logical heading hierarchy
